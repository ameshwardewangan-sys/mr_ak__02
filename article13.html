It is the result of decades of research, innovation, and technological progress. The idea of creating intelligent machines has fascinated scientists for generations. However, the modern journey of AI officially began in 1956, when researchers introduced the term Artificial Intelligence during the famous Dartmouth Conference in the United States. At that time, computers were extremely slow and had limited memory. Even simple calculations required large machines that occupied entire rooms. Despite these limitations, researchers believed that one day computers would be able to think, learn, and solve problems like humans. Although that dream seemed impossible at the time, today's AI systems have already proven that many of those early predictions were correct. --- 🕰️ Timeline of AI Development 1950–1970: The Beginning During the early years, scientists focused on creating programs that could solve mathematical problems and play simple games. Major achievements included: - Early machine learning concepts - Logic-based reasoning systems - Chess-playing computer programs - Initial natural language experiments However, computers lacked the power needed for advanced AI. --- 1970–1990: The AI Winter Expectations for AI became very high, but technological limitations slowed progress. Problems included: - Slow processors - Limited data - High development costs - Weak algorithms Funding decreased, and many AI projects were cancelled. This period became known as the AI Winter. --- 1990–2010: AI Returns As computers became faster and the internet expanded, AI research accelerated again. Important milestones: - Search engines improved dramatically. - Speech recognition became practical. - Digital assistants started emerging. - Machine learning gained popularity. - Data became widely available. This period laid the foundation for modern AI. --- 2010–2025: The AI Revolution This era transformed Artificial Intelligence into a mainstream technology.
